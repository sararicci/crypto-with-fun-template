## üìù Summary
The questions cover four primary topics in Foundations of Cryptography:  
- **Modular Arithmetic Fundamentals**: Concepts of congruence, the role of the remainder, basic modular addition/multiplication, and the finite nature of the set $\text{Z\_n}$.
- **Modular Inverses and Algorithms**: The condition for existence of the multiplicative inverse ($\gcd(a, n) = 1$), finding inverses using the Extended Euclidean Algorithm (EEA), and the use of additive inverses in modular subtraction.
- **Number Theory Theorems**: Application of Little Fermat's Theorem (for prime moduli) and Euler's Theorem (for composite moduli) to simplify modular exponentiation, specifically using $\phi(n)$.
- **Group Theory**: Definitions and properties of a Group (axioms), Abelian Group, and the Multiplicative Group $\text{Z_n}$* (set of invertible elements), including calculating the group order, $\phi(n)$.
